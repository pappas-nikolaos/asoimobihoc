Age and Semantics of Information Workshop

Age and Semantics of Information are of fundamental importance to next-generation communication systems. Age of Information (AoI) is an end-to-end metric that can capture one semantic property known as timeliness or freshness of information, which is important for information-update and data analytics applications (e.g., crowdsourcing, financial trading, social networks, and online learning), as well as networked monitoring and control systems (e.g., sensor networks, airplane/vehicular control, robotics networks, Internet of Things, and Cyber-Physical Systems). Semantics of Information (SoI) captures properties of information such as relevance and importance that relate to the “goal” and “purpose” of information, which is also meant to improve the performance of systems that utilize the information. Recent research advances on the Age of Information suggest that many well-known design principles (e.g., for providing high throughput and low delay) that lead to the success of traditional data networks are inappropriate and need to be re-examined for enhancing information freshness in the rapidly emerging real-time applications. In the meantime, the paradigm shift to Semantics of Information toward 6G communications systems is expected to create systems that generate, process, and transmit only a small fraction of information, which will consume less energy and channel resources without sacrificing their effectiveness. Therefore, the Age and Semantics of Information (ASoI) Workshop will provide a forum where researchers and technical experts can share the latest research insights, present key, and emerging results on the Age and Semantics of Information, and identify new challenges and opportunities.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:
  • Semantics of Information and Task/Goal-oriented Communications
  • Information-theoretic Limits and Interpretations of Age of Information and Semantics of Information
  • Source and Channel Coding for Age of Information and Semantic Communications
  • Random Access and Gossiping Protocols for Age of Information and Semantic Communications
  • Freshness and Semantics of Information in Real-time Learning, Estimation, Computation, and Control
  • Networking Aspects and Protocols for Age of Information and Semantic Communications
  • Resource Allocation and Management for Age of Information and Semantic Communications
  • Modeling and Performance Analysis for Age of Information and Semantic Communications
  • ML/AI Techniques for Semantic and Task/Goal-oriented Communications
